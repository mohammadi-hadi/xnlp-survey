# Data Ethics Reference - Chapter 03: XAI Survey

## Study Type
This chapter presents a **systematic literature review** of Explainable AI (XAI) techniques in Natural Language Processing.

## Data Collection Methodology
No primary data collection was conducted for this chapter. The research methodology consisted of:

1. **Systematic Literature Search**: Querying academic databases (ACL Anthology, IEEE Xplore, Google Scholar, Semantic Scholar) for relevant publications on XAI in NLP
2. **Paper Selection**: Applying inclusion/exclusion criteria to identify relevant studies
3. **Data Extraction**: Extracting methodology details, XAI techniques, and evaluation approaches from selected papers
4. **Synthesis**: Categorizing and analyzing findings across the corpus

## Ethical Considerations

### No Human Subjects
This research did not involve:
- Collection of personal data
- Human participant experiments
- Surveys or interviews
- Processing of sensitive information

### Secondary Data Analysis
All data analyzed in this chapter comes from:
- Published academic papers (publicly available)
- Open-source code repositories (publicly licensed)
- Existing benchmark datasets described in literature

## IRB/Ethics Approval
**Not applicable** - Systematic literature reviews analyzing published research do not require ethics committee approval.

## Data Availability
The primary outputs of this chapter are:
- Taxonomies of XAI methods (presented in Chapter 03 manuscript)
- Comparative analysis tables (included in thesis)
- No raw datasets were generated
